Answers to the most common questions from sports equipment and fitness exhibitors — drawn from 25+ years of experience at the world's leading sporting goods shows.

How do you ship large gym machines and fitness equipment to FIBO Cologne?

Heavy fitness equipment to FIBO Cologne typically travels by LCL or FCL sea freight from Indian ports to Hamburg or Rotterdam, then onward by road to Koelnmesse. We work with our German handling partners to manage move-in under the FIBO official logistics programme. Each machine is disassembled at origin into its component sections, wrapped in stretch film and moving blankets, placed in custom timber crates with steel corner reinforcements, and secured on pallets for racking in the container. Our on-site assembly team receives the cargo at the stand and rebuilds the machines using the original hardware — with a final operational test before the show opens.

Can you handle inflatable sports structures and display arenas?

Yes — inflatable structures are one of our specialities within the sports sector. The inflatable panels or chambers are carefully deflated and folded according to manufacturer guidelines to prevent crease damage to the PVC material, then packed into custom-sewn duffel bags or purpose-made crates. The blower motors and inflation systems travel separately in padded cases. We pre-arrange venue access for the inflation operation — including power supply, floor space, and ceiling clearance checks at the exhibition hall. Our team remains on site to manage the inflation, anchoring, and safe deflation at the close of the show.

What is the process for shipping sports demo equipment temporarily — and bringing it back to India?

Demo equipment — treadmills, cricket nets, smart fitness devices, and so on — is ideally exported under an ATA Carnet, which is an international customs document that allows temporary export and re-import of commercial samples without payment of import duties. STOP India prepares the carnet through the relevant Indian Chamber of Commerce, lists every item with serial numbers and values, and manages the carnet at each border crossing during the journey out and back. Provided the equipment returns to India within the carnet's validity period (typically 12 months), no import duty is payable in either direction. For items with high customs risk, we also offer insurance-backed carnet guarantee services.

How are electronic sports products and e-bikes handled for international shipping?

Electronic sports products — including e-bikes, smart fitness wearables, GPS devices, and electronic scoring systems — often contain lithium-ion batteries which are classified as dangerous goods under IATA regulations. We have DGR-certified staff who assess the battery specification (watt-hour rating), classify the goods correctly, prepare the Shipper's Declaration for Dangerous Goods, and confirm compliance with the airline's acceptance criteria. Batteries that exceed airline limits are separated from the equipment and shipped by sea or road. We never ship non-compliant lithium battery goods by air — the safety and regulatory risk is too high.

What documentation is required to export sports equipment to Germany for ISPO Munich?

For sports goods to Germany (EU), you require: a commercial invoice with accurate HS codes specific to each sports category, a packing list, a certificate of origin (for potential GSP preferential tariff benefit), an ATA Carnet if demo goods are being returned, and any relevant product certification documents (CE marking documentation for certain fitness equipment sold in the EU, BIS certificates where applicable). For goods containing wood packaging, ISPM-15 phytosanitary treatment of all timber packing is mandatory for EU entry. STOP India prepares a show-specific documentation checklist for each ISPO shipment, ensuring nothing is missing that could delay clearance at Munich Airport or cause issues with German customs.

Can you manage our entire ISPO participation — from packing in India to stand setup in Munich?

Absolutely — and this end-to-end management model is exactly what our most experienced sports exhibitors prefer. Our ISPO service covers: pre-show packing inspection at your Indian facility, custom crating and labelling, export documentation and customs clearance in India, air or sea freight to Munich, German customs clearance, Messe München official logistics programme handling, stand delivery on your assigned move-in day, on-site assembly and equipment testing, empty case storage during the show, stand dismantling, re-packing at close of show, and the complete return to India. One point of contact, one invoice, and a team that has done this at ISPO many times before.
