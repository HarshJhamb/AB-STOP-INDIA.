FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions from print and packaging exhibitors.

How do you pack large roll-up banners and signage for transit?

Roll-up banners and flexible signage are wrapped in acid-free tissue and protective plastic film to prevent moisture absorption and creasing. For large rigid signs (metal or composite), we use custom wooden or foam corner protectors and secure wrapping with non-abrasive materials. All items are placed in sturdy cartons or wooden crates with internal padding to prevent movement during transit. We avoid stacking heavy items on top of flexible materials, which can cause permanent creases.

What's the maximum size display you can ship and install?

Our standard capability is displays up to 25 metres long × 10 metres high. For specially oversized structures, we conduct structural engineering assessments, arrange specialized flatbed transport, and coordinate rigging with venue technical teams. Weight capacity on standard flatbeds is typically 25–30 tonnes, but we can arrange heavy-haul transport for equipment exceeding this. Each large-format project is assessed individually to ensure safe transport and professional installation.

Do you handle printing equipment and machinery shipments?

Yes. We have extensive experience shipping printing presses, packaging equipment, converting machines, and industrial display units. These items require specialized handling due to their weight, size, and sometimes their mechanical sensitivity. We arrange proper crating with internal bracing, secure transport on flatbeds with load restraints, and coordinate installation with technicians at the venue. For machinery requiring setup or calibration, we coordinate with your technical team for post-delivery commissioning.

How do you protect printed materials from moisture damage?

Printed materials are vulnerable to humidity and water damage. We use moisture-barrier films, silica gel packs, and acid-free tissue to create a protective envelope around all shipments. All cartons and wooden crates are sealed to prevent moisture ingress. For especially valuable or long-transit shipments, we use climate-controlled containers and can install humidity monitors to track conditions throughout the journey. At destination, items are stored in climate-controlled venues until collection.

Can you arrange on-site installation and rigging support?

Yes. STOP India provides professional installation crews at venues for unloading, assembly, rigging, and stand setup. Our teams work with venue technical coordinators to ensure safety compliance and precise installation. For complex structures requiring specialized skills (electrical rigging, LED panel mounting, heavy equipment alignment), we coordinate with certified technicians and provide full supervision. This service is particularly valuable for large displays and exhibition stands that require professional handling.

What documentation is required for printing ink or solvent shipments?

Printing inks, solvents, and some coatings are classified as dangerous goods (Class 3 — flammable liquids). These require IATA/IMDG declarations, proper hazmat packaging (UN-certified containers), dangerous goods labels, and special handling documentation. We prepare all required declarations, ensure proper packaging according to IATA regulations, and coordinate with airlines that accept these shipments. For shipments combining hazmat items with non-hazardous goods, we separate and package accordingly to comply with all regulations.
