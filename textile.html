FAQ

Questions
We're Often
Asked

Answers to the most common questions from textile and home furnishing exhibitors — drawn from 25+ years of experience at the world's leading shows.

How do you prevent garments from wrinkling during international air freight?

Garment care starts at packing. For designer collections and high-value pieces, we use hanging garment cartons — tall corrugated boxes with integrated hanging bars that keep garments suspended throughout transit. For flat items, we layer acid-free tissue between each piece, use anti-crease cardboard supports, and select carton sizes that prevent garments from shifting. We also advise on stacking limits per carton and ensure nothing sits under heavy cargo in the aircraft hold. For ultra-premium collections destined for shows like Première Vision, we can arrange dedicated garment handling at the airline terminal.

What is the best way to ship large carpet rolls to Domotex or Heimtextil?

Carpet rolls require specialist handling at every stage. We roll each carpet around a solid cardboard or PVC core, apply a felt underpad to protect the pile, and wrap in polyethylene to prevent moisture ingress and soiling. Depending on size and weight, rolls travel by air (for lighter, shorter pieces) or sea freight in open-top containers with custom dunnage. For Domotex specifically, we have established relationships with Hannover's official venue handlers, who have the equipment to move heavy carpet rolls safely through the halls without dragging or folding. We always book cargo-capable vehicles for last-mile delivery of oversized rolls.

Can unsold fabric samples be returned to India without paying duty again?

Yes — provided the correct temporary export documentation was prepared at the outset. For samples shipped under an ATA Carnet, the return to India is covered within the carnet's validity period at no additional duty cost. For samples exported under standard shipping bill with the intent to re-import, we file a drawback or RODTEP claim on the outbound journey and manage the re-importation under the duty exemption for returned goods. STOP India prepares the correct documentation at the time of export — not as an afterthought — so that the return journey is always administratively clean.

How do you handle colour-sensitive or dye-prone fabrics during packing?

Colour separation is a standard part of our textile packing protocol. Dark and heavily-dyed fabrics are individually wrapped in acid-free tissue or polyethylene before any contact with lighter pieces. We never pack dark and pale fabrics in the same layer without separation barriers. For indigo-dyed and natural-dye products — which are particularly prone to transfer under pressure — we use individual poly-bags per piece. We also control humidity in storage, as moisture dramatically accelerates dye migration. These protocols are especially important for shows like Heimtextil where your samples will be displayed under strong lighting and handled by buyers.

What documents do we need to export textile samples to Germany for Heimtextil?

For textile samples to Germany (an EU member state), you typically require: a commercial invoice with declared value and accurate HS codes, a packing list, a certificate of origin (Form A or EUR.1 for preferential tariff under the India-EU GSP scheme, or a standard COO), an ATA Carnet if samples are being returned, and in some cases a phytosanitary certificate for natural fibre products containing plant material. STOP India prepares a complete documentation checklist specific to your product categories and ensures all certificates are obtained and verified before the shipment departs India — avoiding costly customs delays at Frankfurt Airport.

Can you manage both the fabric samples and our stand display materials in one shipment?

Absolutely — and we strongly recommend consolidating your fabric samples, stand displays, lighting equipment, brochures, and promotional materials into a single managed consignment wherever possible. This reduces overall freight cost, simplifies customs documentation, and means a single on-site supervisor receives everything at the venue. We are specialists in mixed-cargo textile consignments, handling fabric rolls, hanging garments, hard-sided display fixtures, and printed materials together in a carefully planned single shipment — each category packed and documented correctly within the same consolidated piece.
