What Our On-Site Team Handles

From the moment your cargo arrives at the venue gate to the final move-out, our supervisors manage every ground operation.

Last-Mile Delivery Management

Our supervisor receives your shipment at the venue loading dock, verifies contents against the packing list, and coordinates delivery directly to your designated stand position within the hall.

  • Cargo receipt & verification
  • Dock-to-stand coordination
  • Delivery scheduling within move-in windows

Empty Case Storage

Once your stand is set up, empty crates and packaging must be removed promptly. Our team labels, collects, and stores all empty cases in the venue's dedicated storage area — and returns them precisely when move-out begins.

  • Case labelling & cataloguing
  • Venue storage coordination
  • Timely return for move-out repacking

Vendor & Handler Liaison

We communicate directly with official venue logistics contractors, stand builders, and freight handlers on your behalf — resolving conflicts, accessing restricted areas, and navigating organiser regulations efficiently.

Setup & Dismantling Oversight

Why Empty Case Management Is More Critical Than It Looks

At major trade fairs, thousands of exhibitors are moving in simultaneously. Venue storage space is tightly controlled, access times are strictly enforced, and uncollected empty cases can result in fines or, worse, cases being disposed of by venue staff.

Our supervisors know exactly how each venue's empty case system works — from Messe Frankfurt to the Dubai World Trade Centre — and manage the labelling, tagging, and placement process with precision so nothing goes missing.

Our Empty Case Process:
  • Unique label per case with exhibitor name, stand number & case number
  • Photo record of all cases before storage removal
  • Coordination with official venue empty case contractor
  • Timed return matching your move-out access slot
  • Full inventory cross-check on return from storage

Frequently Asked Questions

Common queries about our on-site supervision service — answered.

How early does the supervisor arrive at the venue?

Our supervisor typically arrives at the venue the day before move-in begins, or at the very latest, early on the first move-in morning. This allows time to collect exhibitor credentials, confirm stand location, meet with organiser logistics staff, and be fully briefed before your cargo arrives at the loading dock.

Can the supervisor assist with stand construction or electrical work?

Our supervisors specialise in logistics management — cargo receipt, delivery, empty case storage, and coordination. Physical construction work is typically the domain of your stand builder. However, our supervisor can coordinate directly with your stand builder and the official venue handlers to ensure they have timely access to your materials and tools.

What happens if cargo is delayed and arrives after the move-in window?

This is exactly where having a supervisor pays off. Our team will immediately liaise with the venue to request an access extension or alternative delivery slot, escalate with the carrier for priority delivery, and communicate all updates to your team. We have managed late shipments at some of the world's busiest venues and always find a workable solution.

Do you provide supervision for multi-hall or multi-pavilion participations?

Yes. For large or complex participations across multiple halls or pavilions, we scale our team accordingly. We can deploy multiple supervisors or a lead supervisor with supporting assistants, ensuring every stand location has dedicated coverage throughout move-in, the show, and move-out.

Will I receive updates while the show is running?

Absolutely. Our supervisor provides regular updates by email or messaging platform throughout move-in and move-out. During the show itself, they remain available and contactable at all times. At the conclusion of the event, you receive a full written supervision report with photographs covering all key activities and cargo status.
