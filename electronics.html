FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The most common questions from technology exhibitors — answered directly.

Can you ship lithium batteries by air freight to an international exhibition?

Yes, but lithium batteries — whether standalone or contained in equipment — are classified as dangerous goods under IATA regulations and require specific packing instructions (PI 965–970 depending on type) and documentation. STOP India has trained dangerous goods staff who manage lithium battery shipments correctly, selecting airlines that accept DG cargo, preparing the required Shipper's Declaration, and ensuring state-of-charge compliance. We handle this routinely for consumer electronics, EV tech, and battery prototype exhibitors.

What is an ATA Carnet and should we use one for our exhibition samples?

An ATA Carnet is an international customs document that allows temporary exportation of goods to participating countries without paying import duties — on the condition that the goods are returned within the agreed period. For exhibition samples and demo equipment that will come back to India, an ATA Carnet is usually the most cost-effective solution. STOP India assists with Carnet preparation through the appropriate issuing body, manages Carnet stamps at each border crossing, and tracks return deadlines to avoid penalty. We advise on whether a Carnet is preferable to temporary importation under bond based on the destination country and product type.

How do you protect prototype technology from damage or theft during transit?

Prototype protection starts at packing — we use custom-fabricated foam inserts, hard-shell cases, tamper-evident seals, and GPS trackers inside the consignment for high-value pieces. The shipment travels under a seal-to-seal chain of custody with every seal break documented. At destination, cargo is held in bonded storage and released only to a named recipient with identity verification. We can also arrange for an STOP India supervisor to personally escort ultra-high-value or sensitive prototype shipments from origin to stand on request.

Do you handle the return of unsold demo units and exhibition equipment?

Absolutely — return logistics is a core part of our service. After the show, our on-site supervisor oversees repacking of all demo units and exhibition equipment using the original crates or new specialist packaging. We conduct a physical inventory reconciliation before sealing, arrange return customs documentation (including Carnet re-importation stamps where applicable), and despatch the consignment back to your Indian warehouse. Post-show logistics is often where less experienced forwarders fail — we apply the same rigour as the outbound shipment.

Can you handle oversized or heavy technology like industrial robots or server racks?

Yes. We regularly handle oversized and overweight technology cargo for industrial exhibitions like Hannover Messe, Automation Expo, and similar events. This includes route surveys for road transport, special permits for oversized loads, crane and rigging coordination at the venue, and on-site assembly support logistics. For very heavy items such as machine tools or industrial robots, we work with specialist heavy-lift contractors in the destination country to ensure safe installation at your stand.

What insurance options are available for high-value electronics in transit?

Standard freight carrier liability is typically insufficient for high-value electronics — carrier limits may cover only a fraction of the actual value. STOP India arranges comprehensive all-risk exhibition cargo insurance from leading underwriters, covering the full declared value of your goods from origin warehouse to exhibition stand and back. Policies can be tailored to include accidental damage during installation, theft on-site, and mysterious disappearance. We strongly recommend dedicated cargo insurance for any technology shipment valued above ₹10 lakh, and our team will guide you through the declaration and claims process if required.
