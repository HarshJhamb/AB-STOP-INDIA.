The ATA Carnet Explained

The international passport for your exhibition goods — and why expert handling matters at every stage.

What Is an ATA Carnet and Why Does It Matter?

The ATA Carnet (Admission Temporaire / Temporary Admission) is an international customs document that allows goods to be temporarily exported and re-imported across over 80 countries without paying customs duties or taxes at each border.

For exhibitors, it is the single most important customs tool — allowing you to take your stand, products, and promotional materials overseas and bring them back duty-free, provided the carnet is correctly managed at every stage.

However, a single missed endorsement, an incorrect goods description, or a late re-export can result in the full duty and tax value of your cargo becoming payable — sometimes running into lakhs or crores of rupees.

Common ATA Carnet Mistakes We Prevent
  • Missing or incomplete border endorsements at exit
  • Goods descriptions not matching the carnet list
  • Re-export after the carnet validity deadline
  • Failure to account for goods sold or given away at the show
  • Transit stamps missed at intermediate country borders

How We Manage Your ATA Carnet — Step by Step

1
Goods List Preparation

We work with your team to compile a detailed, accurate goods list — including descriptions, quantities, values, and weights — that matches exactly what will be physically presented at each border crossing.

2
Carnet Application & Issuance

We coordinate with the issuing chamber of commerce (such as FICCI or ICC India) to prepare and issue the ATA Carnet in time for your departure, including arranging any required security deposit or guarantee bond.

3
Outbound Export Endorsement

At the port of exit in India, our customs team presents the carnet to authorities, obtains the required export endorsement, and ensures all counterfoils are correctly stamped and retained for the return journey.

4
Destination Import Endorsement

Our destination partner presents the carnet at the country of entry, secures the temporary import endorsement, and manages any additional country-specific requirements for exhibitors arriving under carnet cover.

5
Transit Stamps (if applicable)

For shipments transiting through intermediate countries, we ensure every transit border correctly stamps the appropriate carnet counterfoil — a step often overlooked that can trigger duty claims later.

6
Re-Export Discharge & Closure

After the show, we manage the re-export endorsement at the destination country's exit port and the corresponding re-import at the Indian border — formally discharging the carnet and protecting you from any residual duty liability.

Temporary Imports Without an ATA Carnet

Not every country accepts the ATA Carnet, and not every exhibitor has time to obtain one before their first international show. In these cases, a Temporary Import Permit (TIP) or a customs bond arrangement provides an alternative route for duty-free temporary admission of exhibition goods.

Navigating TIP procedures requires detailed knowledge of local customs law, the right local customs broker, and flawless documentation. STOP India's established partner network handles this across even the most complex destinations — from certain African markets to some Asian countries where carnet coverage is limited.

Our Temporary Import Handling Includes:
  • Assessment of best customs route per destination
  • TIP application with local customs authorities
  • Duty bond or guarantee arrangement where required
  • Full compliance with re-export timelines and conditions
  • Coordination with local customs broker at destination

Customs & Documentation — FAQs

Answers to the questions exhibitors ask most frequently about international customs procedures.

How far in advance should I apply for an ATA Carnet?

We recommend starting the ATA Carnet process at least 3 to 4 weeks before your planned shipment date. This allows time to compile the accurate goods list, make any corrections, obtain the carnet from the issuing chamber, and arrange the security deposit or guarantee. For first-time applicants or complex goods lists, allow additional time. Contact us as soon as your exhibition is confirmed.

What goods cannot travel under an ATA Carnet?

ATA Carnets are designed for goods intended for re-export — they cannot be used for consumable items (including food, printed brochures intended for distribution, promotional giveaways, or goods intended to be sold at the show). Perishables, postal traffic, and goods to be processed or repaired are also excluded. We review your full goods list at the outset to identify which items can travel under the carnet and which require alternative customs treatment.

What happens if some goods are sold or given away at the show?

Goods listed on an ATA Carnet that are not re-exported become liable for import duty and taxes in the destination country. If you plan to sell or distribute goods at the show, we separate these from the carnet inventory before departure and arrange appropriate commercial import documentation for those items. This requires careful pre-planning — inform us at the briefing stage of anything you intend to leave behind.

Can you handle customs for countries that don't accept ATA Carnets?

Yes. For countries outside the ATA Carnet system, we arrange Temporary Import Permits through our local customs partners. The process and documentation requirements vary significantly by country, which is why early engagement with our team is essential. We have handled temporary imports in markets including Saudi Arabia, certain African nations, and other countries with complex admission requirements for exhibition goods.

What is ISPM-15 and does it affect my exhibition shipment?

ISPM-15 (International Standards for Phytosanitary Measures) is an international regulation requiring wooden packaging materials — including crates, pallets, and dunnage — used in international trade to be heat-treated or fumigated and marked with the IPPC stamp. Most countries enforce this for exhibition shipments. We check your packing materials for ISPM-15 compliance during our pre-shipment review and arrange treatment if required, preventing costly holds at destination borders.

How do I know my ATA Carnet has been properly discharged after the show?

We track every carnet from issuance through to final discharge. Once the goods have been re-exported and the corresponding re-import in India has been completed, we obtain and retain copies of all endorsed counterfoils. We send you a formal discharge confirmation and retain the file for a minimum period in line with carnet validity. You will never be left uncertain about whether your carnet obligations have been fully met.
