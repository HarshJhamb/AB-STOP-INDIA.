FAQ

Commonly Asked
Questions

Do you need an import licence to exhibit a medical device in the UAE?

Yes. The UAE Ministry of Health requires that medical devices brought into the country — even for trade fair demonstration purposes — have an active product registration or a temporary import permit issued by the MoH. STOP India assists exhibitors in applying for the relevant MoH permission before the shipment departs India. For devices that are not yet UAE-registered, we can apply for a temporary demonstration permit. Failure to obtain this permit can result in the consignment being held at Dubai Customs, which is particularly damaging at a show like Arab Health with strict move-in windows.

Can pharmaceutical samples with controlled substance status be shipped to overseas exhibitions?

This is one of the most complex areas in medical exhibition freight. Controlled substances — narcotics, psychotropics, and certain prescription-only drugs — require export authorisation from India's Ministry of Health (NDPS permits) and import authorisation from the destination country's competent authority. The processing time for these permits can be 4–8 weeks, so early planning is essential. In many cases, exhibitors choose to display empty or replica packaging at the exhibition and keep only non-controlled demo samples. STOP India advises on the most practical approach based on the specific substances involved and the destination country's regulatory environment.

How do you maintain cold chain integrity during customs clearance at the airport?

Cold chain management during customs is critical — a consignment of biologics sitting in a non-refrigerated customs warehouse can breach temperature specifications in hours. STOP India pre-lodges all customs documentation before the aircraft lands so clearance begins the moment the cargo arrives. We use customs agents who have dedicated cool room facilities airside at major pharmaceutical cargo hubs, ensuring goods transfer directly from aircraft cold hold to controlled storage. For 2°C–8°C products, we also carry excess dry-ice or gel pack duration to accommodate delays. Full temperature records are provided in the handover documentation.

What documentation is required for sterile medical devices going to Medica Germany?

For sterile medical devices entering the EU for exhibition at Medica Düsseldorf, you typically require: CE Mark Declaration of Conformity, Authorised EU Representative letter, commercial invoice with correct Chapter 90 HS codes, packing list, ATA Carnet (if samples are returning to India), and for Class IIb and III devices, a copy of the Notified Body certificate. Post-Brexit, UK-registered devices need a new EU REP. STOP India prepares a destination-specific documentation checklist for every EU exhibition shipment and can advise on any recent changes under the EU MDR which has specific transition timelines still affecting some device categories.

Can you manage the disposal of expired or unused pharmaceutical samples after the show?

Yes. Post-show disposition of pharmaceutical samples is a regulatory matter — you cannot simply discard them. Options include returning them to India (subject to re-import rules and CDSCO requirements), transferring them to a licensed local pharmaceutical company, or arranging compliant destruction through a licensed pharmaceutical waste contractor in the host country. STOP India coordinates whichever disposal route is regulatory-compliant and most practical, ensuring you leave the exhibition with a complete chain-of-custody record for all samples — inbound and outbound. This is particularly important for controlled substances where full accounting is mandatory.

What is SCOMET and does it apply to medical devices?

SCOMET (Special Chemicals, Organisms, Materials, Equipment and Technologies) is India's export control list, maintained by the DGFT. It applies to items that have dual-use potential — civilian and military applications. Some medical and life sciences products fall within SCOMET categories, including certain biological agents, diagnostic equipment with radiological components, and specific chemical precursors used in pharmaceutical manufacturing. If a product is listed under SCOMET, an export authorisation from DGFT is required before it can leave India. STOP India conducts a SCOMET screening on every medical exhibition consignment and advises on whether authorisation is required, managing the application process if needed.
