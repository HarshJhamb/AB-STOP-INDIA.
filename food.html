FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions from food & beverage exhibitors.

Can you ship frozen food samples by air freight for an exhibition?

Yes. Frozen food samples can be shipped by air using dry ice (CO₂ solid) or gel pack insulated shippers depending on the transit time and required temperature. We prepare the packaging to meet IATA Packing Instructions P203 for dry ice shipments, obtain the required dangerous goods declaration, and select flight routings that minimise time in transit. For shipments requiring consistent -18°C to -25°C, we select airlines with proven cold-chain capabilities and often use dedicated reefer ULD containers for larger quantities.

What documentation is required to import food products into the UAE for Gulfood?

For food imports into the UAE, you typically require a commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, health certificate (issued by the exporting country's competent authority), halal certificate (mandatory for meat and poultry), and an import permit where applicable. For Gulfood, the Dubai Municipality also requires specific labelling compliance in Arabic and English. STOP India prepares a complete documentation checklist based on your specific product category and ensures all certificates are obtained from the correct issuing authorities before shipment.

How do you ensure the cold chain isn't broken during customs clearance?

Cold chain management during customs is one of the most critical — and often mishandled — stages. STOP India pre-lodges all customs documentation before the aircraft lands, so the clearance process can begin the moment the shipment arrives. We use customs brokers with dedicated cold store facilities at major cargo hubs, ensuring your goods go directly from the aircraft into cold storage rather than sitting on a warehouse floor. For especially time-sensitive consignments, we can arrange priority handling with airline ground handlers in advance.

Do you provide temperature monitoring certificates for our records?

Yes. For all temperature-sensitive consignments we use calibrated data loggers that record temperature at regular intervals throughout the journey. On delivery, you receive a full temperature log report showing min, max, and average temperatures across the entire transit. This is valuable for your own quality assurance records and, in the event of any product quality dispute, provides evidence of temperature compliance from our end of the chain.

Can unsold exhibition samples be returned to India after the show?

Return of food samples to India is possible but subject to Indian import regulations, FSSAI requirements, and phytosanitary rules for certain product categories. In many cases, exhibitors choose to either donate unsold samples, dispose of them at the venue, or in some categories export them to a third country. STOP India advises on the most practical and cost-effective post-show disposition of unsold food samples based on the product type, host country, and Indian import rules — and we arrange all necessary logistics for whichever route is chosen.

How far in advance should we book for major shows like Gulfood or Anuga?

For major food shows, we strongly recommend contacting us at least 8–12 weeks before the exhibition, and for frozen or complex product categories, 12–16 weeks is preferable. Early booking allows us to secure cold store facilities at the destination, complete all documentation preparation without last-minute pressure, and provide the most competitive freight rates. For shows like Gulfood, which attracts thousands of food exhibitors from India alone, capacity for cold chain transport can tighten significantly in the weeks before the event.
