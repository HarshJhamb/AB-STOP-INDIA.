Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about return exhibition logistics — answered.

When should I book return logistics?

Ideally, return logistics should be booked at the same time as your outbound shipment. This ensures we can plan move-out slots, pre-arrange carriers, and prepare all return documentation well in advance. Booking close to the show end-date can result in delays or higher costs due to carrier availability.

What happens to empty crates during the show?

Our on-site team manages empty case storage throughout the show. We collect empty crates immediately after setup, store them in the designated venue storage area, and return them to your stand at the start of move-out — so repacking can begin immediately.

Can you handle partial shipments if some goods are sold at the show?

Yes. We routinely handle situations where some goods are sold, donated, or disposed of at the venue. We update the inventory, revise the customs documentation, and arrange return shipping only for the remaining items — ensuring full compliance without over-reporting.

How do you handle ATA Carnet re-exportation?

Our customs specialists handle ATA Carnet re-export stamping at the port of exit in the host country, and co-ordinate the matching re-import declarations with your customs broker back home. We track the carnet validity dates to ensure no penalties arise from late re-exportation.

What if some items are damaged during dismantling by venue staff?

Our team documents and photographs all items before and after repacking. We recommend cargo insurance for every return shipment and can assist with claim filing in the event of damage. Our presence on-site acts as an important layer of protection and evidence if disputes arise.
